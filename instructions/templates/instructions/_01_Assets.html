There are two assets in this experiment: CASH (valued in points) and STOCK.

Each period CASH will earn interest of 5% and each unit of STOCK that you own will earn a dividend of either 40 points or 100 points. Either dividend amount is equally likely and past dividends do not affect the dividend amount for the current period.

Suppose you ended the trading period with 12,000 points in CASH and 5 units of STOCK, and the dividend is 40 points per share. The CASH will earn 12,000 x .05 = 600 points in interested, and the STOCK will earn 5 x 40 = 200 points. Your resulting CASH amount will then be

12,000 + 600 + 200 = 12,800 points

.

If you are trying to earn the most money, you might consider whether holding CASH or buying STOCK will create the most earnings. Depending on the amount of CASH and STOCK that you have, interest might exceed dividend earnings or vice versa.
