When you short the STOCK, you will need to have a sufficient cash buffer. As stated before, short positions are shown as a negative number of shares. Multiplying by the current market’s price gives the value of your short position. If this value is more than 50% of you cash position, that will trigger an automatic buy-in. You will be given a warning at the start of the trading period. If you are unable to buy back enough STOCK on your own, the system will submit a BUY order sufficient to bring the value of your short position to a below the 50% threshold.

Suppose you start a trading period with 6000 in CASH and you have shorted the STOCK by 2 shares (that is, your STOCK position is -2). If the current market price is 1501 points, then the value of your short is 3002 points or just over 50% of your CASH.

At the end of the period, if you have not been able to buy back enough stock, then the system will attempt to purchase stock on your behalf.

These automatic buybacks can occur at very high prices so it’s best to try to maintain the proper CASH buffer.
