Equity is the combined value of your CASH and STOCK.    Shorting the STOCK or borrowing CASH will decrease your equity.    If you decide to short or borrow, you have taken on debt. The ratio of equity to debt is called your MARGIN, and you will be required to maintain a margin of {{ marg_ratio_pct }} of your debt.

If the price of the STOCK is 3000 points, and you have shorted it by 2 shares, then the value of your STOCK is -6000 points.    If you have 10,000 in CASH, your total equity is 4000   (10,000 - 6000).

Your MARGIN is 67% of the value of your STOCK    (4000 / 6000).    This is well above the {{ marg_ratio_pct }} margin requirement.

If the price of the STOCK is 2000 points, and you own 7 shares, then the value of your STOCK is +14,000 points.    If you have also borrowed 10,000 points of CASH, your equity is 4000 points (14,000 - 10,000).

Your MARGIN is then 40% (4000 / 10,000) which is below the {{ marg_ratio_pct }} margin requirement. At this point you will need to sell off shares of STOCK to bring up your MARGIN.
